GOTS Certification

Global Organic Textile Standard

The Global Organic Textile Standard (GOTS) is the world's leading textile processing standard for organic fibers, including ecological and social criteria, backed by independent certification of the entire textile supply chain.

Why GOTS Certification?

  • Access to premium international markets
  • Enhanced brand reputation and consumer trust
  • Compliance with global sustainability standards
  • Competitive advantage in the textile industry

Who Needs GOTS?

  • Textile manufacturers using organic fibers
  • Chemical suppliers to the textile industry
  • Exporters targeting eco-conscious markets
  • Brands committed to sustainable practices

Our comprehensive approach to GOTS certification ensures a smooth and efficient process, minimizing disruption to your operations while maximizing your chances of success.

3

Certification

Coordination with certification bodies, audit preparation, and support during the certification process.

Key GOTS Requirements

Environmental Criteria

  • Organic fiber content requirements (min. 70% for "made with organic")
  • Restricted chemical inputs according to toxicity and biodegradability
  • Wastewater treatment requirements
  • Energy and water usage monitoring
  • Waste management policies

Social Criteria

  • Compliance with ILO core labor standards
  • Fair wages and working hours
  • No child labor or forced labor
  • Safe and hygienic working conditions
  • Freedom of association and collective bargaining

ZDHC Compliance

Zero Discharge of Hazardous Chemicals

The Zero Discharge of Hazardous Chemicals (ZDHC) Programme is a collaboration of brands, value chain affiliates, and associates committed to eliminating hazardous chemicals from the textile, leather, and footwear value chain.

Why ZDHC Compliance?

  • Meet requirements of major international brands
  • Reduce environmental impact of chemical usage
  • Improve worker safety and health conditions
  • Enhance brand reputation and market access

Who Needs ZDHC?

  • Chemical manufacturers and suppliers
  • Textile dyeing and processing facilities
  • Leather tanneries and processors
  • Suppliers to major international brands

ZDHC Programme Components

MRSL

Manufacturing Restricted Substances List defines chemical substances banned from intentional use in facilities processing textile materials.

Wastewater Guidelines

Standards for wastewater quality that define expectations for hazardous chemical discharge from textile processing facilities.

Chemical Management

Framework for chemical management systems to ensure proper handling, storage, and use of chemicals in production processes.

ISO Certifications

International Organization for Standardization

ISO certifications are internationally recognized standards that ensure your organization meets specific requirements for quality, environmental management, and occupational health and safety.

Why ISO Certification?

  • Enhance operational efficiency and productivity
  • Demonstrate commitment to quality and sustainability
  • Meet customer and regulatory requirements
  • Improve market access and competitive advantage

Who Needs ISO?

  • Manufacturing companies of all sizes
  • Chemical producers and suppliers
  • Textile processing facilities
  • Organizations seeking operational excellence

ISO Standards We Support

ISO 9001:2015

Quality Management System standard that helps organizations ensure they meet customer and regulatory requirements while continuously improving their processes.

ISO 14001:2015

Environmental Management System standard that helps organizations minimize their environmental impact and comply with applicable laws and regulations.

ISO 45001:2018

Occupational Health and Safety Management System standard that helps organizations reduce workplace risks and create safer working conditions.

ISO 17025:2017

Testing and Calibration Laboratories standard that specifies the general requirements for the competence, impartiality, and consistent operation of laboratories.

ISO 27001:2013

Information Security Management System standard that helps organizations manage the security of assets such as financial information and intellectual property.

Integrated Management Systems

Combined implementation of multiple ISO standards (e.g., ISO 9001 + ISO 14001 + ISO 45001) for a comprehensive management approach.

REACH Compliance

Registration, Evaluation, Authorisation and Restriction of Chemicals

REACH is a European Union regulation concerning the Registration, Evaluation, Authorisation and Restriction of Chemicals. It addresses the production and use of chemical substances, and their potential impacts on both human health and the environment.

Why REACH Compliance?

  • Access to European Union markets
  • Legal compliance for chemical substances
  • Enhanced product safety and quality
  • Competitive advantage in global markets

Who Needs REACH?

  • Chemical manufacturers and importers
  • Companies exporting to EU markets
  • Downstream users of chemicals
  • Distributors of chemical substances

REACH Key Components

Registration

Companies must register chemical substances manufactured or imported in quantities of 1 tonne or more per year with the European Chemicals Agency (ECHA).

Evaluation

ECHA evaluates registration dossiers to check compliance and may request additional information to clarify potential risks to human health or the environment.

Authorisation

Substances of Very High Concern (SVHC) require special authorization for continued use, ensuring adequate control of risks and substitution when suitable alternatives exist.

Restriction

Certain dangerous substances may be restricted or banned from use in specific applications to protect human health and the environment.

MSDS Preparation

Material Safety Data Sheets

Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDS) or Safety Data Sheets (SDS) are comprehensive documents that provide detailed information about chemical substances and mixtures, including their properties, hazards, handling, storage, and emergency measures.

Why Professional MSDS?

  • Legal compliance with international regulations
  • Enhanced workplace safety and health protection
  • Improved emergency response capabilities
  • Better supply chain communication

Who Needs MSDS?

  • Chemical manufacturers and suppliers
  • Companies handling hazardous substances
  • Exporters to international markets
  • Distributors and retailers of chemicals

MSDS/SDS 16-Section Format

Section 1: Identification

Product identifier, supplier details, recommended use

Section 2: Hazard Identification

Classification, label elements, other hazards

Section 3: Composition

Information on ingredients and impurities

Section 4: First Aid Measures

Emergency procedures for exposure

Section 5: Fire-Fighting Measures

Extinguishing media and special hazards

Section 6: Accidental Release

Personal precautions and cleanup methods

Section 7: Handling & Storage

Safe handling and storage conditions

Section 8: Exposure Controls

Occupational exposure limits and PPE

Section 9: Physical Properties

Physical and chemical characteristics

Section 10: Stability & Reactivity

Chemical stability and reactive hazards

Section 11: Toxicological Info

Health effects and exposure routes

Section 12: Ecological Info

Environmental effects and fate

Section 13: Disposal

Waste treatment and disposal methods

Section 14: Transport Info

Shipping classifications and requirements

Section 15: Regulatory Info

Safety and environmental regulations

Section 16: Other Information

Additional relevant information
